Description:

  • The study addresses the critical issue of high dropout rates in freemium role-playing games by analyzing how player motivationsâ€”measured through engagement time, collaboration, and achievementsâ€”affect retention across different game levels.
  • A generalized linear mixed model (GLMM) with a shared parameter structure is developed to jointly model player behaviors and dropout (churn), capturing level-wise dynamics and dependencies, with scalable estimation via a Hamiltonian Monte Carlo algorithm.

A Large-scale Constrained Joint Modeling Approach For Predicting User Activity, Engagement And Churn With Application To Freemium Mobile Games.
  • Category: Digital Gaming
  • Link to pdf
  • Citation text:
    Banerjee T, Mukherjee G, Dutta S and Ghosh P. A Large-scale Constrained Joint Modeling Approach For Predicting User Activity, Engagement And Churn With Application To Freemium Mobile Games. Journal of American Statistical Association, 2019.

Description:

  • The paper introduces the Constrained Extremely Zero-Inflated Joint (CEZIJ) model to jointly analyze player activity, engagement, and churn in mobile freemium games, capturing their complex interdependencies and handling highly zero-inflated data.
  • CEZIJ integrates domain-specific structural constraints and performs high-dimensional variable selection using a distributed, split-and-conquer computation strategy, enabling scalable analysis and superior prediction accuracy.
  • The model identifies co-dependent player behaviors and uncovers clusters of churn risk profiles, allowing for more effective player segmentation and targeted strategies to enhance retention and monetization.

Bootstrapped Edge Count Tests for Nonparametric Two-Sample Inference Under Heterogeneity.
  • Category: Healthcare
  • Link to pdf
  • Citation text:
    Banerjee T, Bhattacharya B and Mukherjee G. Bootstrapped Edge Count Tests for Nonparametric Two-Sample Inference Under Heterogeneity. Journal of Computational and Graphical Statistics, 2024.

Description:

  • The paper addresses the limitations of existing nonparametric two-sample tests when applied to large, heterogeneous datasets with latent subpopulations, which can lead to misleading conclusions.
  • A new nonparametric testing procedure is introduced that accounts for latent heterogeneity by modeling a composite null with identical component distributions but varying mixing weights. It uses a bootstrap-based calibration for practical implementation.
  • The method shows robust performance in both simulations and real-world applications, such as detecting anomalous user behavior in online games, making it suitable for diverse, complex data environments.

A nearest-neighbor based nonparametric test for viral remodeling in heterogeneous single-cell proteomic data.
  • Category: Healthcare
  • Link to pdf
  • Citation text:
    Banerjee T, Bhattacharya B and Mukherjee G. A nearest-neighbor based nonparametric test for viral remodeling in heterogeneous single-cell proteomic data. Annals of Applied Statistics, 2020

Description:

  • Traditional nonparametric two-sample tests fail to account for latent subpopulations in heterogeneous single-cell protein expression data, leading to unreliable conclusions about post-infection changes.
  • The study introduces Testing Remodeling under Heterogeneity (TRUH), a nonparametric test based on composite nulls and nearest neighbor projections, designed to detect true infection-driven changes while adjusting for hidden heterogeneity.
  • TRUH shows strong nonasymptotic and asymptotic performance, and its application to HIV-infected tonsillar T cells aligns with known biological insights, outperforming traditional methods that lack heterogeneity correction.

A scalable path algorithm for clusterwise messaging component analysis in digital communications.
  • Category: Digital Promotions
  • Link to pdf
  • Citation text:
    Bhuyan R, Kar W, and Mukherjee G. A scalable path algorithm for clusterwise messaging component analysis in digital communications.

Description:

  • The study introduces CURM, a statistical framework that segments users based on historical data and provides fine-grained, cluster-specific insights into how different messaging components influence consumer responses.
  • CURM incorporates short-term engagement shifts and recent message effects using generalized clusterwise linear regression, with parameters estimated via a scalable Gibbs sampling algorithm leveraging Polya-Gamma augmentation for binomial outcomes.
  • Implemented in the PACURM R package, the method is applied to digital couponing in the apparel industry, showing how segmentation, messaging frequency, and content significantly impact campaign effectiveness.
